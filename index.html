NOAA/ National Weather Service Heat Index
Checker

What is this tool?
This tool calculates the Heat Index — how hot it actually feels — combining air temperature (°C) and relative humidity (%). Enter your values below to determine the current thermal comfort level.

How to use
  1. Enter the current air temperature in degrees Celsius (°C).
    Example: 35
  2. Enter the relative humidity as a percentage (%).
    Example: 70
  3. Click 🔥 Check Heat Index to compute the result.
  4. The result box will show the Heat Index value and its danger level based on NOAA / NWS guidelines.
  5. Click ↺ RESET to clear all fields and start over.

Note: Best used when temperature is 20°C or above and humidity is 30% or higher.
